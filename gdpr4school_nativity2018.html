#GDPR4School - School Nativity - Resolve any potential GDPR issues before they take to the stage

It is almost that time of the year again when your most endearing pupils will take the stage to perform their nativity play. The school will be filled with proud parents, their smart phones, tablets and cameras. They will be taking pictures of their loved ones and their peers, which could then be shared……anywhere..

You may be well on track with costumes, songs and dress rehearsals, but have you thought about pre-empting a discussion on who makes the pictures or records the video before all hell breaks loose?

At GDPR4school we provide dedicated GDPR knowledge towards the education sector. In the instance of “pictures and parents” we see three options for pre-empting a privacy discussion:

Option 1: Do not allow for any pictures to be taken during any play in your school

In case of heated discussions or previous social media mishaps, your safest option may be not to allow parents to take any pictures during a play. By choosing this option you will gain time that you would have otherwise spent on discussions, but parents will lose an opportunity to create an important memory of Little Johnny doing his first performance as King 3.

Option 2: Arrange for a person that can take pictures during any play in your school

By choosing this option you will allow for pictures to be taken, but not by the parents. You will arrange for one of your staff or a photographer to take pictures during the play and distribute them afterwards. But realise that ‘afterwards’ should be in time for Aunt Rosie to see them!

Option 3: You gather explicit consent well in time before the performance You gather explicit consent well in time before the performance

If you have collected consent for photographing the students during school activities but sense that not all parents want their child photographed during a school activity that includes other parents, then option 3 may be a solution. In this option you create a form for explicit consent to take pictures or record a video during activities that include other parents. Each parent will be required to sign the form well in time before the performance, let’s say one week in advance.

If parents do not allow their child to be photographed by another parent, then this will leave you with enough time to rearrange the stage. Children whose parents have consented may go to one side, children whose parents have not consented go to another side.

Does this sound over complicated? Then limit photography until after the performance. You will still have the guarantee that the children can be photographed on stage with a smile and their beautiful costumes.
